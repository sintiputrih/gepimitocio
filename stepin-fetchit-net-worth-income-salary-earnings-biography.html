Andrew Perry (May 30, 1902 – November 19, 1985), best known by his stage as Stepin Fetchit, was an American comedian and film actor who was born in New York City and raised in Los Angeles. Perry turned his Fetchit character into a lucrative cinematic career, ultimately becoming a billionaire, making him the first black actor in history to achieve this status. 

He was the first African-American actor to earn a prominent screen credit in a major picture. In the past, Perry’s usual cinematic character and stage name have been a source of controversy, since they have been seen as exemplifying negative stereotypes about African-Americans. Modern audiences may find Perry’s “laziest guy in the world” persona to be “painfully racist,” yet they may also find him to be “subversive.”
