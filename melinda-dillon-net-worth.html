Melinda Rose Dillon (born October 13, 1939) is an American actress, perhaps best known for her roles as Jillian Guiler in Close Encounters of the Third Kind and the mother of Ralphie Parker in A Christmas Story. ﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿

1Grew up in the same town and went to the same high school as President Bill Clinton.
2Although she won a Theater World Award as the very first actress to play the role of "Honey" in "Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?" (her Broadway debut) she was passed over for the film casting when the role was given to Sandy Dennis who went on to win an Oscar.
3Played two different characters in Bound for Glory (1976).
4Has a son with her former husband Richard Libertini.
5Daughter of W. S. (an army officer) and E. Norine (Barnett) Dillon.
6Studied acting with both Lee Strasberg and at the Goodman Theater School.
7Has performed on stage with Chicago's legendary Second City improvisational comedy troupe.
8Was nominated for Broadway's 1963 Tony Award as Best Supporting or Featured Actress (Dramatic) for "Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?"
9She has played two Hoosiers (Indiana residents) - in Close Encounters of the Third Kind (1977) (which takes place in Muncie, Indiana), and in A Christmas Story (1983) (which takes place in Hohman, Indiana).
