Early Life and Career Exploits

Ons Jabeur was born in a small town in Tunisia known as Ksar Hellal.

From the age of four to thirteen, Jabeur trained with coach Nabil Mlika; she first began working with him in a tennis promotion center at her school.

She could only practice on courts at surrounding hotels when she was 10 because her club lacked its own tennis courts.

Jabeur relocated to Tunis’s capital city when she was twelve years old to train at the Lycée Sportif El Menzah, a national sports high school for the nation’s aspiring athletes, where she stayed for a number of years.

From her humble beginnings, and with hard work, she has made a name for herself in the tennis world.

She reached her highest WTA (Women’s Tennis Association) ranking of No. 2 on June 27, 2022.

The highest-ranked African and Arab tennis player in WTA and ATP rankings history is Jabeur, who is currently rated No. 1 in Tunisia.

She has won eleven singles championships and one doubles title on the ITF Circuit in addition to four singles titles on the WTA Tour.

In 2022, Jabeur finished second at Wimbledon and the US Open, making history as the first African and Arab woman to compete in a Major singles final.

Ons Jabeur began regularly participating on the WTA Tour in 2017 after spending nearly ten years playing primarily at the ITF level.

In 2019, she received the Arab Woman of the Year Award.

The first African or Arab woman to make it to a major quarterfinal was Jabeur at the 2020 Australian Open, a feat she replicated at the 2021 Wimbledon Championships.

She also made history by winning the 2021 Birmingham Classic, becoming the first woman from Africa or the Arab world to do so.

Jabeur became the first female African, Tunisian, and Arab player to triumph at this level when she won the 2022 Madrid Open, a WTA 1000 competition, which was also her best victory to date.

Her accomplishments are attributed to increasing tennis’ popularity across the African continent.
