The top 5 worst losses in the History of the Dallas Cowboys

The Dallas Cowboys, a prominent American football team, have had their fair share of victories and defeats throughout their illustrious history. However, among their many triumphs, there have been several painful losses that have left a lasting impact on the team and its fans. Let's delve into the top 5 worst losses in the history of the Dallas Cowboys.

1. Super Bowl V - January 17, 1971

The Dallas Cowboys suffered a devastating loss in Super Bowl V against the Baltimore Colts. The game was marred by turnovers and penalties, with the Cowboys committing a record 10 turnovers. The culmination of errors led to a 16-13 defeat, including a game-winning field goal by the Colts with just five seconds remaining. This loss was particularly heartbreaking for the Cowboys and their fans as it marked their first Super Bowl appearance and ended in a disappointing fashion.

2. 1981 NFC Championship Game - January 10, 1982

The Cowboys faced a crushing defeat in the 1981 NFC Championship Game against the San Francisco 49ers. With less than a minute remaining, the Cowboys held a 27-21 lead and seemed poised to secure the win. However, a dramatic turn of events saw Joe Montana connecting with Dwight Clark for "The Catch," a game-winning touchdown that dashed the Cowboys' hopes of reaching the Super Bowl. This loss was a bitter pill to swallow for the Cowboys and their supporters, as they were just moments away from clinching victory.

3. 2007 Divisional Round - January 13, 2008

In the 2007 Divisional Round, the Cowboys suffered a demoralizing loss to the New York Giants. Despite being the favorites, the Cowboys were unable to contain the Giants' offense, ultimately falling 21-17. This defeat was particularly painful as the Cowboys had enjoyed a successful regular season, only to falter in the playoffs. The loss marked another disappointing end to a promising season for the Cowboys and left their fans disheartened.

4. 2014 Divisional Round - January 11, 2015

The 2014 Divisional Round saw the Cowboys endure a heartbreaking loss to the Green Bay Packers. With contentious calls and missed opportunities, the Cowboys fell short, losing 26-21. The defeat was especially agonizing as the Cowboys had shown resilience throughout the game, only to have their hopes dashed in the final moments. This loss left a lingering sense of frustration for the Cowboys and their supporters, as they came tantalizingly close to advancing further in the playoffs.

5. 2016 Divisional Round - January 15, 2017

In the 2016 Divisional Round, the Cowboys suffered a gut-wrenching loss to the Green Bay Packers. Despite a valiant effort, the Cowboys were unable to overcome the Packers, ultimately losing 34-31. The defeat was particularly painful as it marked another missed opportunity for the Cowboys in the playoffs. The loss left the team and its fans grappling with the disappointment of falling short once again when it mattered most.

The Dallas Cowboys have experienced their fair share of heart-wrenching losses throughout their history. While these defeats have undoubtedly been difficult to endure, they have also served to highlight the team's resilience and the unwavering support of their dedicated fan base. Despite these painful setbacks, the Cowboys continue to strive for excellence and remain a formidable force in the world of American football.
