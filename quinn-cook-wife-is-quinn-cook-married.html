Who Is Quinn Cook?

Quinn Cook is a basketball player and former point guard for the Zhejiang Lions of the Chinese Basketball Association (CBA).

Born on March 23, 1993, in Washington DC, had a successful collegiate career playing for the Blue Devils at Duke University, where he scored scored 14 points as a freshman.

Prior to that time, Cook played high school basketball at DeMatha Catholic High School in Hyattsville, Maryland.

Cook was not drafted in the 2015 NBA draft, so he played Summer League basketball for the Oklahoma City Thunder and the Cleveland Cavaliers.

Cook later went on to play for the Canton Charge, Dallas Mavericks, New Orleans Pelicans, Golden State Warriors, Santa Cruz Warriors, Los Angeles Lakers, Cleveland Cavaliers, Lokomotiv Kuban, and the Stockton Kings.

In 2022, Cook signed a deal with the Zhejiang Lions of the CBA, and went on to score a CBA career-high of 54 points in just 29 minutes during a game against the Shandong Heroes.

So far, Cook has won two NBA championships – one with the Golden State Warriors and the other with the Los Angeles Lakers.
