Who voiced Mama Coco? Meet Ana Ofelia Murgua

Remembering the Voice Behind Mama Coco: Ana Ofelia Murguía’s Artistic Journey

“Coco,” the heartwarming Pixar film that celebrated Mexican culture and explored the bonds of family, captivated audiences around the world. At the heart of the film was the character Mama Coco, voiced by the talented actress Ana Ofelia Murguía.

The Voice of Mama Coco

Ana Ofelia Murguía, born on December 8, 1933, in Mexico City, became a household name for her iconic portrayal of Mama Coco in Pixar’s “Coco.”

Educational Background

Ana Ofelia Murguía’s journey into the world of acting began with formal education.

She studied acting at Mexico’s National School of Theater Arts, where she honed her skills and laid the foundation for a prolific career in the performing arts.

Debut and Prolific Career

Murguía made her debut in the world of acting in 1954, marking the commencement of a journey that spanned over six decades. Throughout her illustrious career, she graced the stage in more than 70 plays and showcased her talent on the silver screen in over 90 films.

A Versatile Artist

Known for her versatility, Ana Ofelia Murguía seamlessly navigated between the realms of theater and film, showcasing her ability to embody a diverse range of characters.
