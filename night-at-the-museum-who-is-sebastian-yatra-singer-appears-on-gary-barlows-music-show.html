Night At The Museum: Who is Sebastian Yatra? Singer appears on Gary Barlows music show! Sun

Gary Barlow has announced Night At The Museum, a one-off musical special filmed in London’s Natural History Museum. The set-list has been revealed and there’s one singer people want to know more about!

Colombian singer Sebastian Yatra is taking the world by storm after being set to appear on Gary Barlow’s festive programme Night At The Museum.

Read on to find out more about Sebastian Yatra and get the lowdown!

View Instagram Post

Night At The Museum: Who is Sebastian Yatra?

Sebastian was born in Medellin, Colombia, and moved to Miami when he was 5 years old. He learned to speak English fluently and grew up singing. Growing up he studied piano and guitar and was an enthusiastic footballer before deciding to pursue music as a profession. He began composing in 2013, he has now collaborated with the Jonas Brothers and performed with Halsey at the Latin Grammys last year.

 His single “Traicionera” earned him numerous awards, including a HEAT Latin Music Award and a Shock Award along with other award nominations. Sebastian used to be in a relationship with Tini Stoessel. It was obvious after she appeared in Sebastian’s music video for his single, “Christina,” that the couple were an item. In June 2019, Sebastian and Tini made it Instagram official, when Sebastian shared a photo of the couple kissing with the caption, “Cada vez mas [amor] meaning “every time more love”.

However the couple called it quits, Sebastian and the Argentine pop star made the announcement to fans on May 16th on their Twitter accounts, expressing that they had decided to end their relationship.

Singer appears on Gary Barlow’s music show!

Along with Sebastian Yatra, other guests who will appear are Michael Bublé, Boyzone star Ronan Keating, Jack Whitehall, Jason Donovan, Alesha Dixon, Alfie Boe, Beverley Knight.

Gary Barlow spoke about his programme and said: “I am so excited to host this music special. During a tumultuous time for the whole country, it feels amazing to be able to put on a proper night of entertainment at one of the most exciting locations in London and featuring some amazing guests and musicians.”

He continued: “It’s going to be a very special evening and even though we are staying inside at the moment, let’s get our glad rags on and make believe we’re heading out for the night.”

Gary Barlow’s Night At The Museum will air on ITV at 9pm on Sunday 6th December. We can’t wait to tune in!
