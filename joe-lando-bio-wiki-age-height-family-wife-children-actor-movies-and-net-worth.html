Joe Lando Biography

Joe Lando, born Joseph John Lando (born December 9, 1961) is an American actor, most recognizable for playing Byron Sully on the TV series Dr. Quinn, Medicine Woman. Lando’s debut role was that of a shore patrolman in the 1986-released blockbuster hit American science-fiction film ‘Star Trek IV: The Voyage Home.’ He continued appearing in bit roles in the series ‘Nightingales’ (1989) and ‘Beauty and the Beast (1990) and in the black comedy film ‘I Love You to Death’ (1990).

    Joe Lando Actor

    He got noticed by casting directors after his portrayal of ‘Jake Harrison,’ one half of the iconic couple ‘Jake & Megan,’ in the ‘ABC’-aired soap opera ‘One Life to Live,’ from 1990 to 1992. This helped him earn the most recognizable role of his career, a rugged outdoorsman named ‘Byron Sully,’ in the Western drama series ‘Dr. Quinn, Medicine Woman.’

    His character was featured in 143 episodes of the series, from 1993 to 1998, as the love interest of ‘Michaela Quinn,’ played by Jane Seymour. In 1993, he found a place on ‘People’s list of the ‘50 Most Beautiful People in the World.’

    Following this, he reprised the role of ‘Sully’ in the spin-off series of ‘Dr. Quinn, Medicine Woman,’ titled ‘California’ (1997); in the movies ‘Dr. Quinn, Medicine Woman: The Movie’ (1999) and ‘Dr. Quinn: The Heart Within’ (2001); and in the short parody ‘Dr. Quinn, Morphine Woman’ (2014). The last film, produced by ‘Funny or Die,’ saw Lando reuniting with Jane Seymour and many other ‘Dr. Quinn’s cast members.

    Meanwhile, he was featured in several other productions, such as the TV series ‘Guiding Light’ (1993) and ‘JAG’ (1998), the TV films ‘Shadows of Desire’ (1994) and ‘Alien Nation: The Enemy Within’ (1996), and the feature films ‘Seeds of Doubt’ (1996) and ‘No Code of Conduct (1998).

    The 2000s

    He starred as ‘Peter Scarbrow’ in the American–Canadian drama series ‘Higher Ground,’ created by Michael Braverman and Matthew Hastings. He also worked as an executive producer for the series. The series originally aired on the ‘Fox Family’ network for a season, from January 14, 2000, to June 16, 2000. Lando also appeared in the TV films ‘Bloodsuckers’ (2005) and ‘Engaged to Kill’ (2006), directed by Hastings.

    The 2003 American made-for-TV disaster film ‘Killer Flood: The Day the Dam Broke’ featured him in the starring role of ‘David Arthur Powell’ along with actors Michele Greene and Bruce Boxleitner. The following year, he appeared in a small role, along with Lori Loughlin, in a single episode of the American TV drama series Summerland.’

    He appeared in seven episodes of the series ‘Wildfire’ (2005), eight episodes of the supernatural teen drama series ‘The Secret Circle’ (2012), and two episodes of the sports drama series ‘Hit the Floor’ (2014). He also appeared in a TV movie for ‘PixL,’ titled ‘Casa Vita’ (2016).

    Some of his recent roles have been those of ‘David Dematti’ in the 2010-released American science-fiction film ‘Meteor Apocalypse’ and ‘Special Agent John Samson’ in 2017 direct-to-video action film ‘Sniper: Ultimate Kill.’

    He wrote the 2016 short film ‘Spotlight 2.’
