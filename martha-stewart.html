Stockbroker career

In the year 1967, Martha Stewart began a second career as a stockbroker. Meanwhile, her husband Andrew founded a publishing house and served as chief executive of several others.

Likewise, In the year  1976, Stewart started a catering business in her basement with a friend, Norma Collier. The business took off well and became successful.

Following her catering success, She released many more books under the Clarkson Potter publishing imprint.

Similarly, some of her books are Stewart’s Quick Cook (1983),  Stewart’s Hors D’oeuvres (1984), and Martha Stewart’s Pies & Tarts (1985).

In addition to cookbooks, she also wrote wedding planning and entertainment. The books are titled Weddings (1987), The Wedding Planner (1988), and Stewart’s Secrets for Entertaining (1988).

Moreover, she also released holiday and Thanksgiving cookbooks and are titled Martha Stewart’s Quick Cook Menus (1988), and Stewart’s Christmas (1989)

Following her success in the cooking industry, she bought some media houses. The businesswoman merged them into a new company, named Martha Living Omnimedia (MSLO).

Similarly, Martha served as chairwoman, president, and CEO of the new company.

Likewise, On October 19, 1999, Martha’s company went public on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol MSO. The company’s IPO was set at $18 per share and jumped to $38 by the end of trading.

As her company made good progress in the stock market, Stewart became a billionaire on paper. Moreover, she is also the first female self-made billionaire in the U.S.
